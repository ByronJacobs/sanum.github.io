History

The SANUM meeting came into existence in 1975 when two eminent mathematicians, Lothar Collatz (Hamburg) and Fritz John (New York), happened to visit South Africa at the same time. A number of local researchers used the opportunity to organize a meeting at the (then) University of Natal, at which a total of ten papers were presented. From here the meeting grew in strength, with numerous famous numerical and applied mathematicians passing through. Aside from missing a single year (1999), the meeting has been held annually, which makes 2016 the 40th anniversary of this major event in the calendar of South African numerical and applied mathematics.
